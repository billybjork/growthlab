## 10â€“25 min Â· The Current AI Landscape

**Goal:** Understand what AI tools exist and what they're actually good at.

---

**Three broad categories of AI tools:**

1. **Text-based** (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)
   - Fast ideation, copywriting, brief generation

2. **Image-based** (Midjourney, DALL-E, Stable Diffusion)
   - Visual exploration, concept art, asset generation

3. **Video-based** (Runway, Synthesia, emerging tools)
   - B-roll generation, editing, voiceover synthesis

---

**Important reality check:**

> Prompt: "What AI tools do you already use in your daily workflow, and where do you see gaps?"

These tools are **best for:**
- Generating variations quickly
- Overcoming creative blocks
- Handling repetitive, templated work

These tools are **NOT good for:**
- Brand consistency (yet)
- Complex narratives
- Client-specific customization (without significant refinement)

## 40â€“55 min Â· Constraints & Creativity

**Goal:** Understand how constraints paradoxically unlock creativity with AI.

---

**The precision spectrum:**

AI tools work best when you give them clear boundaries. Too vague, and you get generic. Too constrained, and it's just busywork.

Examples of **vague briefs:**

> "Make a cool design for a tech startup."

vs. **Precise briefs:**

> "Create a black-and-white minimalist logo for a fintech app that helps freelancers track invoices. Style: flat, no gradients, modern sans-serif."

---

### Activity: Precision Spectrum Mapping

In your breakout rooms (3-4 people each):

1. You'll find a FigJam board linked in Slack
2. I'll provide 5 brief examples (ranging from vague to precise)
3. **Your task:**
   - Place each brief on the spectrum
   - Rate which ones are "AI-ready" vs. which need refinement
   - Discuss: Why does precision matter for AI prompts?

*Timeboxed: 10 minutes, then we'll discuss as a group.*
